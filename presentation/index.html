What is blockchain?

What is blockchain?

Blockchain is a chain of blocks with information

Blockchain is a database consisting of a sequential chain of digital blocks, with information about the previous and next blocks

Each block has transaction data

Each block, in addition to information about the transaction, has its own unique hash

Each block has its own unique hash

and changing a block will cause its hash to change, which means it will invalidate other blocks

The block cannot be modified

Advantages

  • Improved accuracy by removing human involvement in verification
  • Cost reductions by eliminating third-party verification
  • Immutability of recorded data
  • Decentralization makes it harder to tamper with
  • Transactions are secure, private, and efficient
  • Transparency of data

The properties of blockchain make the technology unique in terms of security. The main advantage of blockchain lies in its transparency, as anyone can access information about transactions, while ensuring that no one can modify or destroy it.

Disadvantages

  • Low transactions per second
  • Use in illicit activities(dark web)
  • Regulation remains uncertain
  • Data storage limitations

What are the difficulties?

The three main components are:

  • Security
  • Scalability
  • Decentralization

You can only choose two components

There are three main tasks for the development of a blockchain network - scalability trilemma. Vitalik Buterin first wrote about it in detail. You cannot achieve everything at once; you can only choose two components. To ensure scalability, you have to sacrifice something.
